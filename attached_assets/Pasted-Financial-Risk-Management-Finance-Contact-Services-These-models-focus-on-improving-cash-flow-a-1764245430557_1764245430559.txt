Financial & Risk Management (Finance & Contact Services)
These models focus on improving cash flow and reducing bad debt, which is crucial in the high-value construction sector.

Credit Risk Scoring (Contact Service):

Data: Customer demographics, industry, past payment history (balance_due, payment_terms compliance from Finance Service).

ML Model: Classification model (e.g., Logistic Regression or Random Forest).

Output: A risk score (e.g., 0 to 100) or a classification (Low, Medium, High Risk).

Action: When a Sales Rep creates a large quote, the CRM instantly displays the customer's risk score, guiding the decision to accept payment terms or require a larger deposit.

Cash Flow Forecasting (Finance Service):

Data: Historical invoice due dates, actual payment dates, seasonal patterns, and upcoming project milestones.

ML Model: Time series forecasting (e.g., ARIMA or Prophet).

Output: Predicts future cash inflows based on invoice due dates and predicted payment delays.

Action: Provides the Finance team with a more accurate cash flow report than simple averages, enabling better resource planning and management of ZAR liquidity.

These models focus on accurate sales predictions and managing pipeline health.

Lead Scoring and Prioritization (Sales Service):

Data: Lead source, industry (construction sub-sector), size of the opportunity, and historical conversion rates for similar leads.

ML Model: Classification model (e.g., Gradient Boosting).

Output: Assigns a score (e.g., 0-99) to each Lead or Opportunity.

Action: Sales Reps prioritize only high-scoring leads, focusing effort where conversion probability is highest, improving sales efficiency.

Project Completion and Delay Prediction:

Data: Historical project data (scheduled vs. actual completion dates), resource utilization, weather data, and the complexity score of the project.

ML Model: Regression or time series model.

Output: Predicts the probability of a project milestone being delayed by more than X days.

Action: Proactive alerts to the Site Manager and Operations Specialist to re-allocate resources before a delay occurs.

3. ðŸ“¦ Operations and Inventory Optimization (Inventory Service)
These models are vital for controlling material costs and ensuring necessary items are on site when needed.

Predictive Maintenance (HR/Operations Services):

Data: Usage logs for key equipment (cranes, excavators), inspection dates, and historical failure records (linked to the equipment's UUID).

ML Model: Survival analysis or classification.

Output: Predicts the probability of equipment failure in the next 30 days.

Action: Triggers a Purchase Order (PO) for maintenance parts or schedules a preventative service ticket, minimizing expensive, unplanned downtime on a construction site.

Demand Forecasting (Inventory Service):

Data: Historical material usage per project type, seasonal demand (e.g., more cement in the dry season), and upcoming Sales Orders.

ML Model: Time series forecasting.

Output: Predicts the future demand for key materials (e.g., cement bags, rebar) by Site/Warehouse.

Action: Automatically generates optimized reorder points and suggests Purchase Order quantities for the Procurement team, reducing inventory costs while preventing costly stock-outs.
